nockd — dashboard design patterns

5 DIRECTIONS · FLEET + APP DETAIL

Five visual systems for the nockd browser dashboard — the self-hosted control plane for NockApps. Each direction renders the same fleet (apps, artifacts, chain-attach health) and the same app-detail view, so they read as skins of one tool. Type is constant throughout — Jost (geometric sans) for voice, IBM Plex Mono for data — palette and structure carry the difference.

01

Red-Figure

Attic black-glaze ground, terracotta figures, a restrained meander frieze. Table-forward and operational — the pottery palette doing serious sysadmin work.

02

Jasperware

Wedgwood blue ground, white relief. Apps become cameo medallions; data sits on pale tablets. Spacious and editorial — calm enough to read like a museum label.

03

Bauhaus Grid

Buff ground, black structure, primaries used as signal only. Status is geometry — circle, triangle, arrow, square. The most graphic and legible of the five. Table and tile views side by side.

04

LCARS Console

Black ground, elbow frame, stacked pills — but the palette is muted terracotta/wedgwood/olive, not neon. A dense operational cockpit for watching a fleet move.

05

Getty Stone

Travertine and bronze, inscriptional capitals, hairline rules. The artifact becomes a provenance label. The most editorial direction — a gallery wall for a running fleet.
